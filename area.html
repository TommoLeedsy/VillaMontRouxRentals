The Local Area

Grimaud: A medieval hill top village, steeped in history with its chateau and chapel. It is within walking distance or a short drive by car. Grimaud has a full range of shops including a general grocery store, butchers, tabac, gift store and two fabulous boulangeries for those fresh morming croissants and baguettes. Thursday is market day; a great way to sample the real delights of Provence. Grimaud has numerous restaurants, catering for all budgets and gastromonic needs. These include a pizzeria, ice cream parlour, brassieres and more formal a la carte restaurants.Grimaud has a Petit Train which runs throughout the dayduring the summer, from Grimaud to Port Grimaud. This is great fun for children and a fabulous way to travel to the beach.
Port Grimaud: 3 kilometres away. A charming village which is built in Provencale style on a network of canals. From here take the Les Batueaux Verts a regular ferry service to St Tropez. Island tours and fishing trips are also available from here.
St Tropez: 12 Kilometres away. Very popular with the jet set and in crowd. St Tropez is a shopper's paradise with its renowned market on Tuesdays and Saturdays, designer shops and boutiques. The wonderful quay is lined with yachts and gin palaces. A super place to people spot and watch the world go by, in one of the many terrace caf√© and bars. Behind the quay, the small streets and old buildings are very picturesque. There are numerous restaurants to suit all needs.
Beaches: There are numerous wonderful beaches within a short drive from the villa. The nearest one 3 kilometres away at Port Grimaud. The most serious collection of beaches are along the Baie de Pampelonne, south of Saint Tropez and east of Ramatuelle.
